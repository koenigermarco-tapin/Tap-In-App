Rituals that force productive conflict.

Proven Rituals

The "Disagree Round"
Before any major decision, go around the room: "What's wrong with this plan?"

The Pre-Mortem
"Imagine this failed. What went wrong?"

The Devil's Advocate
Assign someone to argue against the proposal

The 10/10/10
"How will we feel about this decision in 10 minutes, 10 months, 10 years?"

Szenario

You're leading a decision. Everyone seems to agree. But you've learned that quick consensus often means hidden disagreement. Best move?

Proceed—if they had concerns they would have said
"Before we commit, let's do a quick pre-mortem. What could go wrong?"
Delay the decision to give people time to think
Send an anonymous poll after to check real agreement
Force the Disagreement
The pre-mortem is powerful because it gives people permission to be negative. It's not "do you disagree?" but "what could go wrong?"—a question everyone can answer without feeling like they're challenging the leader.
