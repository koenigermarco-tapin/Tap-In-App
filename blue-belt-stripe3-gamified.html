The Framework

Radical Candor: The map of feedback.

Kim Scott identified two dimensions that determine whether feedback works:

Care Personally: Do you actually give a damn about the person?

Challenge Directly: Are you willing to tell them the hard truth?

Most people do one. Almost nobody does both.

The Four Quadrants

Radical Candor

Care + Challenge

Obnoxious Aggression

Challenge without Care

Ruinous Empathy

Care without Challenge

Manipulative Insincerity

Neither

Ruinous Empathy is where most of us live. We care, so we stay silent. We don't want to hurt feelings, so we let problems grow.

BJJ Wisdom

In jiu-jitsu, the tap is feedback.

When someone catches you in a submission, you tap. You don't explain why it wasn't really a submission. You don't make excuses. You don't get defensive.

You acknowledge the truth of the moment: they got you.

Then you reset and try again. That's how you get better.

The Practice

When someone gives you feedback, your only job is to say: "Thank you. I'll think about that." Everything else is defense.
