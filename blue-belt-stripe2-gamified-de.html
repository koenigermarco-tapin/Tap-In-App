The Three Levels of Listening

Co-Active Coaching research identified three distinct modes of listening.

1
Internal Listening
Focused on yourself—your reactions, your opinions, what you'll say next
Focus: ME
2
Focused Listening
Focused on the speaker—their words, emotions, body language
Focus: THEM
3
Global Listening
Focused on everything—what's said, unsaid, the energy, the context
Focus: EVERYTHING

Your team member says: "I've been thinking about leaving."

What's your first internal response?

"Oh no, what did I do wrong? How do I fix this?"
"Tell me more about what's going on for you."
"Let's talk about what would make you stay."
Level 1 vs Level 2

The first response is Level 1—you're focused on yourself. The second is Level 2—you're focused on them. The third jumps to solving before understanding.

Most of us default to Level 1. It takes practice to stay in Level 2 and 3.

"Most people do not listen with the intent to understand; they listen with the intent to reply."

— Stephen Covey

Covey's research showed that truly effective people master "empathic listening"—seeking first to understand before being understood.

The 3-Second Rule

After someone finishes speaking, wait 3 full seconds before responding.

This does three things:

1. Signals that you're processing, not just waiting
2. Gives them space to add more
3. Forces you out of Level 1
