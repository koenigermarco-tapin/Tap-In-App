üèóÔ∏è Building Your Philosophy

The five elements of a leadership philosophy.

The Philosophy Framework

1. Core Beliefs

What do you believe about people, work, and organizations? These shape every decision.

2. Non-Negotiables

What will you never compromise on, regardless of pressure? These are your red lines.

3. Success Definition

How do you measure success as a leader? Not what you achieve‚Äîhow you achieve it.

4. Decision Principles

When faced with hard choices, what guides you? These are your tie-breakers.

5. Leadership Practices

What rituals and habits express your philosophy in action? Philosophy must become behavior.

üí° Example Philosophy Statement

Core Belief: "People are capable of more than they know. My job is to create conditions where they discover this."

Non-Negotiable: "I never compromise psychological safety for short-term results."

Success: "I've succeeded when my team can function brilliantly without me."

Decision Principle: "When in doubt, choose the option that builds capability."

Practice: "I start every 1-on-1 by asking 'What do you need from me?' and end by asking 'What should I do differently?'"
