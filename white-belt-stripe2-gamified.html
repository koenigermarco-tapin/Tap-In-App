Vulnerability lives in your body before it reaches your mouth.

Before you speak the hard truth, your body already knows. Learning to read those signals is the first skill.

Think of a time you held back something true. Where did you feel it first?

💗 Chest tightens
🗣️ Throat closes up
🫁 Stomach drops
Face gets hot
🧊 Mind goes blank
Your vulnerability signature

That physical sensation is your early warning system. Most people ignore it or push through. But it's data—your body telling you something important is happening.

The skill isn't suppressing this feeling. It's noticing it, then choosing what to do next.

Both matter. But here's the sharpest test:

"Am I sharing to connect, or to be rescued?"

— The oversharing test

Connection: "I'm going through something hard." Rescue: "I'm going through something hard and now I need you to fix it." One builds bridges. The other creates burden.

The vulnerability hangover is real.

That next-day feeling after you shared something real—the "why did I say that?" spiral. Almost everyone gets it.

When you get it, you usually:

Pull back
Get distant, maybe over-explain or apologize
Stay the course
Let it sit, trust the process
The antidote

The vulnerability hangover usually lies. What felt like "too much" often landed as "finally, something real." Before you spiral, check with one trusted person: "Was that weird?" Usually, it wasn't.

Sometimes openness backfires. Someone uses it against you, or the room wasn't ready.

When that happens, what's the real lesson?

"I shared with the wrong person"
"I shared at the wrong moment"
"I should keep things to myself"
