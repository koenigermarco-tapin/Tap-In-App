Introducing STATE.

A framework for crucial conversations that keeps dialogue safe while getting to the truth.

S

Share your facts

Start with observable, undeniable data—not interpretation

T

Tell your story

Share your interpretation, but own it as YOUR story

A

Ask for their path

Genuinely invite their perspective—they know things you don't

T

Talk tentatively

Confidence in your facts, humility about your conclusions

E

Encourage testing

Make it safe for them to disagree—that's where truth emerges

"Share your facts" is harder than it sounds.

Facts are things a camera could record. "The last three reports came in 2-4 days late" is a fact. "You don't care about deadlines" is a story.

"Ask for their path" requires genuine curiosity.

Not "Do you have anything to say for yourself?" but "Help me understand what's happening from your perspective."
