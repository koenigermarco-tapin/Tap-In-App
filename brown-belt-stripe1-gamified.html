The GROW Model.

Developed by Sir John Whitmore, GROW is the most widely-used coaching framework in the world:

G
Goal
"What do you want to achieve?"
R
Reality
"What's happening now? What have you tried?"
O
Options
"What could you do? What else?"
W
Will
"What will you do? When?"
Apply it
Team member comes to you

"I'm stuck on this project. I don't know what to do next."

Using GROW, what's the best first response?

"Let me take a look. Here's what I'd do..."
"What would success look like for you on this?"
"Have you tried X or Y approach?"
"Don't worry, you'll figure it out."
Start with Goal
Before exploring solutions, clarify what they're trying to achieve. "What would success look like?" helps them define the target. Many people are stuck because they haven't clearly defined what "unstuck" means.

The questions that unlock people.

Power Questions

Goal: "What does success look like?"
"What would be a great outcome?"

Reality: "What have you tried so far?"
"What's really going on here?"

Options: "What else could you do?"
"If you had no constraints, what would you try?"

Will: "What will you do next?"
"What support do you need?"

They've decided. What's your best coaching response?

"Good plan. Let me know how it goes."
"Just be careful—A might not work because..."
"Great. When will you start, and what support do you need from me?"
"Have you considered approach C? It might be better."
Complete the Will
Once they've decided, don't undermine it. Complete the coaching by helping them commit: when, how, and what support. Warning them or suggesting alternatives at this point creates doubt and dependency.
