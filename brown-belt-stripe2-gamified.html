Culture operates on three levels.

Edgar Schein's iceberg model:

1
Artifacts
Visible: Office layout, dress code, rituals, language
2
Espoused Values
Stated: What we say we believe and value
3
Basic Assumptions
Hidden: Unconscious beliefs that drive behavior

Real culture change happens at Level 3. Most initiatives only touch Levels 1 and 2.

Scenario

A crisis hits. You're stressed and snap at someone in a meeting. It's out of character, but it happened. What's the best move?

Move on—everyone has bad days, no need to dwell
Apologize privately to the person later
Apologize publicly to the team—model accountability
Explain the stress to give context
Model the Behavior You Want
Public mistakes require public accountability. When you apologize openly, you model that it's safe to admit mistakes. Private apologies only fix the relationship—public ones shape culture.
